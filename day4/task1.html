ANDRA PRADESH

Andhra Pradesh (English: /ˌɑːndrə prəˈdɛʃ/,[9] Telugu: [aːndʱrɐ prɐdeːʃ] ⓘ abbr. AP) is a state in the southcostal region of india. It is the sevenath-largest state with an area of 162,970 km2 (62,920 sq mi)[3] and the tenth-most populous state with 49,577,103 inhabitants.[2] It shares borders with chhattishgarh, Odisha, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, and the Bay of Bengal. It has the second-longest coastline in India at about 974 km (605 mi).[3] After existence as Andra stateand unified Andhra Pradesh, the state took its present form on 2 June 2014, when the new state of Telangana was formed through bifurcation.[10] Amaravati is the capital of the state, with the largest city being vishakapatnam. Telugu, one of the classical languages of India used by the majority of people, is the first official language. Water sharing disputes and asset division with Telangana are not yet resolved.

As per the 8th century BCE Rigvedic text Aitareya Brahmana, the Andhras left north india off the banks of the Yamuna river and migrated to South India. In the third century BCE, Andhra was a vassal kingdom of Ashoka of the Mauryan Empire. After his death, it became powerful and extended its empire to the whole Maratha country and beyond under the rule of the Satavahana dynasty. After that, the major rulers included the Pallavas, Eastern Chalukyas, Kakatiyas, Vijayanagara Empire, Qutb Shahi dynasty, Nizam dynasty, East India Company, and British Raj.
